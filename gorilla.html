GORILLAS

Gorillas are the largest primates and are closely related to humans, sharing about 98% of our DNA. They are native to the dense forests of central Africa. Gorillas are gentle giants that live in family groups led by a dominant silverback male.

They feed primarily on fruits, leaves, and stems. Gorillas use vocalizations, chest beating, and body language to communicate. Despite their intimidating size, they are shy and peaceful when left undisturbed.

Habitat destruction, disease, and poaching threaten their survival. Mountain gorillas, a critically endangered subspecies, are the focus of major conservation efforts. Gorillas are intelligent and emotional animals, capable of learning sign language and forming deep social bonds.
